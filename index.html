Myths and misconception

Myth 1: Ivermectin can be used to treat Covid-19

Ivermectin is a medication that can be used to treat a wide range of parasitic infestations in both humans and animals, but is mostly known for its use in treatment of livestock.

Claims have recently been pushed by activists and politicians as a potential treatment for Covid symptoms. These claims, however, are not backed by scientific evidence.

Myth 2: Covid-19 vaccines hurt fertility

One argument against vaccination against Covid-19 that’s been spread by some is based on claims that the vaccines hurt human fertility. These claims are false and are not backed by evidence.

#HealthyAtHome

Due to the Covid-19 pandemic, many people are forced to stay at home. This results in reduction of both physical activity and social interaction. These, in turn, result in negative effects on people’s physical and mental health.

Here are some tips to help you to stay healthy during this pandemic:

  1. Eat healthy.
  2. Find a physical activity routine that you can follow at home.
  3. Quit smoking.
  4. Watch your and others’ mental health.
  5. Drink plenty of water.
